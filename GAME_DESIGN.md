# PENINSULAE — Five Nations, One Iberia

A browser RTS about the struggle for self-rule in the Iberian peninsula. Five nations —
**Galicians, Basques, Catalans, Portuguese and Castilians (Spaniards)** — race to unify the
peninsula under their banner. It plays like a compact Age of Empires, but every mechanic is
derived from how these nations actually competed through history: not only with armies, but
with **language, law, trade, faith and the sea**.

## The metaphor → the mechanics

**Historical thesis.** Castile built a centralizing state from the meseta outward. The
peripheral nations endured not mainly by winning battles but by keeping their identity alive —
Galician pilgrimage routes and Atlantic fishing, Basque iron and ancient law (*fueros*),
Catalan Mediterranean trade and civic culture, Portuguese ocean discoveries. Armies took land;
culture kept it.

That thesis becomes the game's central rule:

### 1. Allegiance, not tiles
The peninsula — a 63×46 hex board — is divided into 17 historical
**regions** (Galiza, Asturias, Euskal Herria,
Navarra, Aragón, Catalunya, València, Castela, León, Estremadura, Andalucía, Norte, Lisboa,
Alentejo…). Each has a **village** and an allegiance. You gain a region by:

- **Conviction** (culture): spend **Identity** to start a conversion; a pilgrim-circle of
  influence grows over the village until it raises your banner. Slow, costs Identity, is
  suppressed while enemy soldiers garrison the region ("the garrison forbids the language") —
  but a converted region is loyal: it pays full tribute and resists conquest twice as long.
- **Conquest** (war): destroy the village's guard, hold the village with soldiers until the
  banner falls. Fast — but a conquered region **resents**: it pays reduced tribute and
  enemies can convert it back at a discount. The reconquista cuts both ways.
  (Exact rates live in `src/config/rules.js` — docs stay qualitative so they never drift.)

Owning a region yields its **tribute** (its historical specialty: coastal fish, meseta grain,
mountain ore, trading coasts) and moves your **domination bar**.

### 2. Identity — the fourth resource
Alongside **Food, Wood, Gold** there is **Identity**: generated by your capital, churches
and festival halls, and by each faction's own cultural engine (see bonuses). Identity buys
region conversions and Era advances. It is the currency of nationhood.

### 3. The hegemon dynamic
Castile starts larger (extra regions, central position) with a cheap, fast war machine. For
the four peripheral nations the game **is** an independence race — out-develop the center
before it swallows you. Playing Castile inverts the fantasy: hold the center against four
fires. AI personalities mirror this: Castile expands aggressively by conquest; Portugal creeps
along the coasts; Catalans race economy; Basques turtle and counter; Galicians convert quietly.

### 4. Total domination
The game ends when one nation's banner flies over **every region of the peninsula**. Capturing
a rival's **capital** makes that nation capitulate — its conquered borderlands defect to the
captor (empires fall at the head), but regions it won by conviction shatter back to independence
with fresh village guards: faith does not transfer at swordpoint, so murdering a missionary
nation pays far less than out-believing it. Lose your own capital and your game is over.

## Economy

- **Workers** harvest nodes: **forests** (wood; trees visibly fall to cut stumps), **farms**
  (food, built on grass), **fishing shoals** (food, coastal water), **gold mines** (built
  against mountains). Harvesting is a continuous trickle with floating +N feedback.
- **Buildings** (KayKit medieval set): Capital (trains workers, era advances), House (+pop),
  Farm, Lumber Camp (wood boost), Mine, Market (gold trickle), Church (+Identity),
  Festival Hall (Kingdom era, more Identity), Barracks (soldiers), Archery Range (crossbows),
  Watchtower (auto-fires), Blacksmith (attack/armor upgrades).
- **Population** capped by houses; **construction** shows scaffolding and hammering workers.

## Eras (tech progression)

**County → Kingdom → Golden Age.** Advancing costs Food+Gold+Identity at the Capital,
unlocks buildings/units, and grants the faction's signature tech:

- Galicia **Camiño de Santiago**: the Camino Identity trickle doubles.
- Basques **Foruak**: every unit hardens (bonus armor) — the old law endures.
- Catalans **Consolat de Mar**: the market trickle doubles and conversions get cheaper.
- Portugal **Escola de Sagres**: coastal tribute rises — the ocean pays its captain.
- Castile **Tercios**: soldiers hit harder.

## Combat

Melee soldiers close in and swing (skeletal attack animations, damage keyed to the hit frame);
crossbowmen fire visible bolts; watchtowers volley. Units have HP/armor, play hit reactions,
die with death animations and dust. Buildings burn, crumble to a ruin model. Village guards
(militia + a tower) defend neutral regions.

## Presentation & feel

- Golden-hour directional sun with soft shadows, warm fog, animated sea, drifting clouds.
- Every action answers: selection rings, order pings, hit flashes, floating resource text,
  construction dust, banner-raise beams on region flips, era fanfares, under-attack horn +
  minimap ping + red vignette.
- Quiet medieval folk underscore (real CC recordings), full SFX set (real CC0 recordings).
- Trackpad-first: two-finger scroll pans, pinch zooms, two-finger tap issues orders; WASD
  pans too. Clean HUD: resources+era top-left, **domination race bar** top-center, minimap
  bottom-left, selection panel bottom-center, build menu bottom-right, alerts feed, and an
  in-game **How to play** overlay (H).

## Engineering

- Plain ES modules, no build step; `python3 -m http.server` serves it.
- **Simulation** (`src/sim/`) is three.js-free: fixed 100 ms ticks, hex grid + A*, entities,
  economy, combat, regions, AI — exposes state + an event queue.
- **Renderer** (`src/render/`) consumes sim state read-only, interpolates positions,
  drives AnimationMixers from unit state, spawns effects from sim events.
- **UI/audio** (`src/ui/`, `src/audio/`) also consume sim state/events only.
- `window.__game` exposes sim state for the Playwright verification harness in `tools/`.

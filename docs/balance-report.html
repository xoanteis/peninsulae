WHAT A CLEAN IBERIA REVEALED.

A full audit found the tournament harness itself was biased — era techs leaked across games, gifting Portugal and Catalonia their late-game powers from minute zero — and the AI's personality knobs were partly dead code. With everything fixed, the first honest tournament rewrote the standings overnight. This is the report of the clean world: fair rules, honest telemetry, and a meta that still owes Portugal a future.

Lesson 1 — targeting is the whole game. The baseline's hunt-the-leader rule executed whoever converted fastest (Galicia, dead first in 56/60). Switching to nearest rival just handed the execution to geography: Lisboa and Santiago are the closest capital pair on the map, so Portugal killed Galicia every game and inherited the west — 66.7% overnight. The durable fix (R7) was temporal, not spatial: regicide is an endgame move. No AI now marches on a living nation's castle before the map is carved up, and Galicia's first-fall rate finally moved: 56 → 40.

Lesson 2 — murder must pay less than faith. At baseline, razing a capital transferred the victim's whole empire (winners harvested ~7.5 defected regions per game). Now only conquered borderlands defect; every region won by conviction shatters back to independence with fresh village guards. Winners in the final tournament inherit 1.7 regions per game — capital-killing is a strike, not a lottery.

Lesson 3 — the meta see-saws before it settles. Nerfing the war factions (R1–R4) flipped the game to a two-nation conviction race; R6–R7's war-side levers (conquest tribute 65%, empire fatigue that makes each conversion past your second +12% dearer, tougher fueros) pulled Castile back to 21.7% and spread first-falls across all five nations. R8's extra nudges tested worse (Portugal 61.7%) and were reverted.

Lesson 4 — buff the weakness, not the strength. With R7 shipped, Galicia was still the runt at 5%. The intuitive fix — hand it the same coastal-conversion bonus Portugal enjoys, the two being the peninsula's Atlantic nations — backfired in a full tournament (R9): Galicia already expands fastest, so a coastal discount just made it lead the domination race even earlier and get executed first in 53 of 60 games, with no win-rate gain. Galicia's problem was never expansion; it was survival. R10 gives it a defensive identity instead — a fortress-cathedral capital (Santiago at 4,500 HP, the hardest decapitation strike in the game) and pilgrim watchtowers 35% cheaper, so it can wall the empire conviction wins. Galicia's win rate more than doubled to 11.7%, it now holds 1.3 regions at game's end (was 0.1 — wiped), and by refusing to die it stopped feeding Portugal free meals: Portugal fell from 46.7% to 40.0%. The two Atlantic nations are finally close.

Lesson 5 — follow the gold, not the walls. Doubling down on defense (R11: all Galician buildings +20% HP, towers at half price) made Galicia worse — 1.7%, its old grave. An instrumented probe found why: at minute 12 the AI had raised 3.5 towers and 2.4 soldiers, with 21 gold (zero soldiers in six of eight games). The real bottleneck was never armor — Galicia's Identity economy simply cannot buy soldiers, who cost gold. R12–R13 fix the root cause with As Minas de Gallaecia: rich mine veins and a passive mineral trickle (the Roman gold province; wolfram, tin), plus hardier Irmandiños (+15% HP) and an AI that builds at most three towers after it has an army. Full strength overshot (35%, dethroning Portugal); trimmed, Galicia lands at 20.0% — statistically level with Portugal, Catalonia and Castile. Two caveats worth naming: the granite failure was partly an AI artifact (the fortify rule sank its treasury into towers — a human wouldn't), which is why the AI was fixed rather than the towers; and this tournament measures AI temperaments, not human hands — for a human, cheap towers and a gold income are simply two good tools in the same kit.

Defections — regions inherited when a capital falls — used to reach 7–8 per game for the war factions. The shatter rule caps the harvest; conviction is now the only way to keep what you never garrisoned.

Lesson 6 — the pact, not the march (and know what your tournament measures). The Basques were the last outlier at 5%: gold-rich, iron-tough, and inert — every anti-snowball rule taxed the conquest-and-attrition style they win by. The screens decomposed the fix beautifully: making their AI march without fixing conquest's economics was worse than turtling (0 wins in 20); giving conquest back its profit through the foru pact — regions they take keep their law, pay full tribute, and offer missionaries no grievance to exploit — doubled them to 10%, validated in two independent 60-game runs. Stronger variants (fully-loyal pacts, mines that produce Identity, a higher conviction appetite) all plateaued at screens and were left as inert hooks. And the see-saw turned once more: with the Basques marching in the northeast, Castile bleeds (26.7% → 11.7%) and Portugal breathes (25% → 38.3%) — the watch item for a future round, not a blocker: every nation now wins at or near double digits, and no one dominates like the old duopoly (41.7%) or peak Portugal (71.7%).

Lesson 7 — the Atlantic war, and the limits of measurement. The new kill-graph telemetry showed Galicia and Portugal are each other's apex predators — in the final round, every single Galician victory ran through Lisboa's ashes. R14 left Galicia trailing (its first-falls jumped when the marching Basques stopped busying Portugal), so R15 raised its mineral trickle one notch on a known dose curve (0.18 → 0.25; 0.35 had overshot to 35% months of tuning ago). The rivals now sit level. A caveat the whole series earned: between 60-game runs of near-identical configs, per-faction rates swing as much as ±10 points (Castile measured 11.7% and 28.3% back-to-back) — so the durable claim is the band, not the ordering within it.

Lesson 8 — the bugs were the balance. A four-way code audit found that era techs mutated the shared faction config: inside a tournament process, every game after the first started with Catalonia's Consolat and Portugal's Sagres already active. Every number in this report's earlier rounds was measured on that tilted table. A second silent hand: fallen nations left zombie armies that suppressed conversions and squatted villages — invisible friction that mostly taxed the conviction factions. With both fixed (plus the AI's dead turtle/convictionLove knobs revived), the first honest tournament read Galicia 63.3%, never once killed; Portugal 0%, executed at median minute 11. Ten diagnostic screens rebuilt the meta on the clean foundation: Galicia's nine-bonus kit — over-fitted to a world where it was the eternal victim — was slimmed to five, empire fatigue rose to +25% per region, targeting now sends heat at runaway dominators instead of making big empires mathematically unattackable, the late-game free-for-all waits for minute 15, and Portugal got back the tribute the leak had been paying it illegitimately. The shipped result is fair but not yet flat: Galicia's corner-fortress-convertor remains the strongest AI hand (56.7%), and Portugal — the hegemon's nearest soft target — has not yet won an honest game. Both are documented open problems; the difference is that the next tuning round starts from truth.

R18 — the forests grow back. The first player-driven design change of the live era: cut groves regrow after three and a half minutes at three-quarter stock, so wood-poor nations no longer hit a hard wall in long games. Sixty games say the balance holds — every nation lands inside its R17 interval — and Portugal, for the first time in an honest world, won two games. Castile softened (23.3% → 13.3%, intervals overlapping): an aggressor profits less when its victims' economies grow back behind it.

Where the table stands for a human player: the AI records now describe honest temperaments, not bugs. Galicia is the comfortable pick, Castile and Catalonia the solid mains, the Basques the connoisseur's pact-war — and Portugal, until its rework lands, is the true hard mode: survive Castile's minute-eleven storm with your own hands and the coast is still the richest road in Iberia.

IIIField manuals — how to win each nation now

Updated for the patched rules. The AI record tells you the terrain; these are the lines through it.

Galicians

O Camiño e o Mar

AI record: 34 wins / 60 in the clean world — the strongest hand even after its kit was slimmed; expect a further trim

Opening (min 0–4)
Three Labregos to fish (your +50% makes the coast the best food in the game), two to a forest. Queue workers to ~10. First buildings: Farm → Church (30% off) → House. Convert Norte the moment you can — it's adjacent, its tower becomes yours, and its militia now take up your banner.
As Minas — the gold that buys the war
Gallaecia was Rome's gold province, and it shows: your mines yield +50% and the land itself pays a steady mineral trickle (~11 🪙/min from minute zero). This is the fix for the old Galician disease — an Identity economy that could convert regions but never afford the 20-gold soldier. Spend it: keep a Barracks producing from minute 4, and remember your Irmandiños carry +15% HP — a smaller Galician line trades up against everyone but the Basques.
The sanctuary — your reason to live
Your capital is a fortress-cathedral: 4,500 HP, the hardest decapitation strike in the game, so the focus-fire your early lead attracts breaks on the walls. And your watchtowers are 35% cheaper (39🪵16🪙) — ring your core and each border region with them. You are the one nation that can lead the domination race and survive it. Turtle deliberately: hold, don't chase.
The Irmandades
Every region you convert also hands you its village militia as free, pop-zero home guards — three conversions ≈ a six-soldier garrison you never paid for. They hold their posts and block enemy conquest timers. Behind cheap towers and loyal militia, your converted empire is genuinely hard to unravel.
Middle game
Era up early (Camino doubles: each converted region pays 0.3 📜/s). Chain conversions along the coast. Watch the empire-fatigue tax: past your second region each conversion costs +12% — budget Identity for the region that matters, not the nearest one.
Endgame
You out-last, not out-fight. Loyal regions pay full tribute and resist conquest twice as long, and if a rival dies, their converted lands shatter free for you to re-convert at leisure. When the war nations have bled each other, your crossbows-behind-towers army takes the weakened capital — regicide is safe once the map is carved up, and everything they held by force defects to you.
Mines 1.2 🪙/s per worker (+50%) · mineral trickle 0.25 🪙/s · Irmandiño 109 HP, Besteiro 67 HP (+15%) · fortress capital 4,500 HP · watchtower 39🪵16🪙 (−35%) · fish 1.65 food/s per worker · church 42🪵21🪙 · adjacent conversion 100📜 +12%/region past 2nd · Camino up to 0.3 📜/s per converted region · Irmandades: 2–3 militia (115 HP) per converted region

Basques

Burdina eta Foruak

AI record: 1 win / 60 in the clean world — wedged between the hegemon and the fortress; the deepest challenge run

Opening
You sit on the best mining ground in Iberia. Mine on a mountain edge by minute 2, second mine by minute 5 (+50% gold each, two workers per mine). Wood only for the essentials: Farm, House, Barracks.
The foru — your new engine
Every region you take by conquest signs the foru: no resentment, full tribute, and enemy missionaries get no discount to flip it back. For everyone else conquest is the leaky bucket; for you it holds. March early and often — your +22% HP line wins even trades, and unlike the other war machine (Castile), what you take stays taken and pays in full.
Middle game
Your Aizkolaris carry 116 HP to everyone else's 95 — every equal-numbers melee is a win. Take Nafarroa by conquest early, then re-convert your own conquests: resented lands now pay 65% tribute, but they flip to enemy missionaries at a discount until you make them loyal. Blacksmith both upgrades — toughness compounds with armor.
What changed for you
The old plan — turtle, then decapitate the leader and inherit their empire — is dead. Capital-kills now shatter the victim's converted lands to neutral. So conquer adjacent lands (borderlands still defect to you when a neighbor falls) and keep your army home until the true endgame: your 3,660 HP capital plus two towers makes you the safest late-game power in Iberia.
Endgame
March with 10+ mixed Aizkolaris/Balestaris only after minute 15. Decapitate the nearest weakened rival, take the defecting borderlands, and grind — nobody out-attritions the fueros.
Units ×1.22 HP · buildings ×1.22 HP (capital 3,660) · mines 1.2 🪙/s per worker · era tech: +1 armor

Catalans

Seny i Comerç

AI record: 11 wins / 60 — steady in the pack across every era of the meta; the most consistent nation in Iberia

Opening
Everything you build costs 25% less — so build more of it, sooner. Farm, House, Market inside the first three minutes (market = 53🪵23🪙 for you). Workers to 12 fast.
The core loop
Your market pays per region you hold. Every cheap conversion raises your gold income permanently: take Aragón and València early (both adjacent, both rich). At the Kingdom era, Consolat de Mar doubles the trickle and cuts conversions −25% — which also blunts the empire-fatigue tax that slows everyone else's late conversions.
Defense on discount
Watchtowers at −25% are the cheapest defense in the game: ring your border with them and let attackers bleed on 45🪵19🪙 towers while your economy runs.
Endgame
Out-bank everyone into a late Golden-Age army of Almogàvers, or convert the map: with Consolat, adjacent conversions start at 75 📜. Your loyal empire resists conquest 2× and pays full tribute — the ledger, not the sword, is still the win condition.
All buildings ×0.75 cost · market +0.5 🪙/s +0.12/region (×2 with Consolat) · era tech: conversions −25%

Portuguese

Descobrimentos

AI record: 0 wins / 60 — the honest meta's open problem: Castile's minute-eleven storm finds Lisboa first. In human hands, hard mode with the richest coast in Iberia

Opening
Fast workers (+20%) mean your economy spins up quickest of all. Fish + farms, then a Church by minute 4. Convert Norte immediately — coastal, adjacent, discounted: ~70 📜.
The strategy
Own the ocean rim. Thirteen of seventeen regions touch the sea; every one converts at −30% for you and pays +15% tribute. Chain along the coast, and remember the fatigue tax: each region past your second raises the next sermon's price 12%, so convert the rich coasts first. At the Kingdom era, Sagres adds +50% coastal tribute — the ring becomes the richest empire on the map without one battle.
Military posture
A screening force of 4–6 Cavaleiros walking the coast road suffices (your speed makes one army defend two fronts). Since regicide before the endgame now demands an oversized siege army, your early-game safety is real — spend it on economy.
Endgame
When a weakened rival emerges from the meseta wars, strike their capital from the sea. Their borderlands defect to you; their converted lands shatter — re-convert them at your coastal discount before anyone else raises the Identity.
Workers ×1.2 speed · coastal conversions ×0.7 · coastal tribute ×1.15 (Sagres: +50%) · era tech: coast revealed

Castilians

Plus Ultra

AI record: 14 wins / 60 — the honest hegemon: 10 conquests a game, and almost never the first to fall

Opening
You start with three regions' tribute — spend it. Barracks by minute 3, Tercios flowing by minute 4 (45🌾15🪙 each, 30% faster). Farms on the meseta; your grain tribute carries the food line.
Middle game
Take the center by conquest before minute 10 — 6 Tercios clear a village cleanly, and resented lands now pay you 65% tribute (up from half): empire-building by sword finally cash-flows. As the game's only high-aggression hegemon you may storm capitals from the Kingdom era onward — everyone else must wait for the endgame.
The imperial disease, still
Conquests flip back to enemy missionaries at 40% off. Garrison two units per conquest or pause to re-convert your own lands loyal. And loyal enemy regions resist your sword 2× as long — break nations at the head (their armies and capitals), not region by region.
Endgame
Never duel the Basques on even numbers — their HP wins trades. Out-produce them two-to-one (you can), snipe adjacent capitals for the borderland defections, and mop up the shattered interior with your unmatched production.
Soldiers ×0.75 cost, ×0.7 train time · +2 start regions · resented tribute 65% · era tech: +2 damage, barracks double-queue
